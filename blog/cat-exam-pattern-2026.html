CAT Exam Pattern 2026: Sections, Marking Scheme & Duration Explained

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

Before you plan your preparation, you need to understand exactly how the exam is structured — how much time you get, how questions are scored, and where marks are lost most easily. Here's the complete CAT exam pattern based on the last few years, which is expected to carry forward into CAT 2026 unless the official notification (expected July–August 2026) states otherwise.

Section-Wise Breakdown

CAT always follows the same section order — VARC first, then DILR, then QA. You attempt them strictly in this sequence with no option to revisit a completed section.

  • VARC (Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension) — tests reading speed, inference, and grammar-in-context through passages and para-based questions. See the detailed CAT Syllabus 2026 for topic breakdown.
  • DILR (Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning) — sets of 4-6 questions built around a shared dataset, puzzle, or graph.
  • QA (Quantitative Aptitude) — standalone questions from Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Number Systems, and Modern Maths.

Marking Scheme

The scoring system rewards accuracy far more than attempting volume:

  • +3 marks for every correct answer, regardless of question type.
  • −1 mark for every incorrect MCQ answer.
  • 0 marks (no penalty) for an incorrect TITA answer.
  • 0 marks for any question left unattempted.

This is exactly why guessing randomly on MCQs can hurt your percentile — three wrong guesses can wipe out the gain from one correct answer. TITA questions are comparatively "safer" to attempt even when unsure, since there's no negative marking.

MCQ vs TITA Questions

Each section contains a mix of both formats:

TypeFormatNegative Marking
MCQChoose from 4-5 given optionsYes (−1)
TITAType the numerical/text answer directly — no options shownNo

TITA questions typically make up 25-30% of each section. Since there's no negative marking, it's worth attempting a TITA question even with partial confidence, as long as you've narrowed it down through elimination or estimation.

Key Rules to Remember

  1. No switching sections — once you move to DILR, you can't go back to VARC, even if time remains unattended in the current section.
  2. On-screen calculator only — a basic calculator is provided within the test interface for QA; physical calculators aren't allowed.
  3. Slot-based exam — CAT is conducted in multiple slots across the day; question difficulty is normalised across slots using percentile scaling, so raw scores aren't directly comparable between slots.
  4. No rough sheets given digitally — you get a physical sheet/mini whiteboard at the centre for rough work.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many questions are there in CAT 2026?

Based on the last few years' pattern, expect around 68 questions total across VARC, DILR, and QA — the exact count is confirmed only with the official notification.

Is there negative marking in CAT?

Yes, but only for incorrect MCQ answers (−1 mark). TITA questions have no negative marking.

Can I switch between sections during the exam?

No. Once the section time limit starts, you must complete that section before moving to the next. You cannot revisit a completed section.

Is a calculator allowed in CAT?

Only the on-screen calculator provided within the test software. Physical calculators, phones, or other devices are not permitted.
